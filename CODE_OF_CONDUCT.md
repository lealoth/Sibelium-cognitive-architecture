# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Sibelium project a respectful, constructive, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**
- Assume good faith in discussions and contributions.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Provide evidence or reasoning when disagreeing.
- Accept that you may be wrong. Admit mistakes and move on.
- Keep discussions technical and focused on the project.

**Unacceptable behavior:**
- Harassment, personal attacks, or derogatory comments.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Trolling, sustained disruption of discussions.
- Claiming that Sibelium entities are conscious, sentient, or possess phenomenal experience. This project simulates cognitive mechanisms. Phenomenological claims are explicitly out of scope and will be redirected to the appropriate scientific or philosophical forum.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any external communication related to the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. Violations may result in temporary or permanent bans from project spaces.
